Peanut Butter Cookies

Perfect cookies to make this Fall season!

These classic peanut butter cookies are always a hit with peanut butter lovers of all ages. They're nutty, sweet, and lightly salty with a delicate texture. A big bonus: they're super easy to make and only require a few ingredients.

Incredients

  • 2 cups (9 ounces) all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup (12 tablespoons) unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 3/4 cup peanut butter (smooth or chunky)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup light brown sugar, packed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. In a bowl, stir together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, beat together the butter, peanut butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar for a few minutes until light and fluffy. Beat in the eggs and vanilla until well combined. Gradually beat in the flour mixture just until combined.
  4. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate until chilled, at least an hour or overnight.
  5. Preheat the oven to 375 F and grease 2 large baking sheets. Roll tablespoon-sized balls of dough with your hands and place about 3 inches apart on the baking sheets.
  6. Using a dinner fork dipped in flour, lightly press the cookies, flattening a bit and forming a criss-cross pattern on each cookie.
  7. Bake until golden brown, about 9 to 11 minutes, rotating the pans halfway through baking.
  8. Cool on the baking sheets for 1 minute then transfer to a rack to cool completely.
